# Mean-temperature-change-Ecuador

1. Objective

Analyze the change of mean temperatura in Ecuador, between 2010 and 2024.

2. Data Source

Two dataset were obtained from Clime Engine platform, Terraclime data. The datasets keeps ranges of temperaturas maximums and minimums each one. 

The variables considered were: Mean Temperature and Year.

3. Methods

-Exporting raw CSV files from Climate Engine
-Cleaning and preparation of the CSV fil
-Calculation of mean temperaturas 
-Applied a Linear Regression model to calculate the absolute thermal change (slope-based).
-Visualization of trends using R 


4. Key Findings

Significant Warming: The analysis reveals an absolute increase of 0.98°C in the mean temperature over the last decade.

The 2015 Threshold: A clear shift was observed starting in 2015; since then, annual mean temperatures have consistently stayed above 21.0°C, never returning to previous lower averages.

Conclusion: This near 1-degree increase in such a short timeframe aligns with regional indicators of climate change effects in the tropical Andes.

5. Limitations

Spatial Resolution: Data availability at a provincial or local level in Ecuador is limited within global datasets like TerraClimate, which may overlook microclimate variations.

Data Scarcity: The lack of denser meteorological station networks highlights the need for more robust local databases to conduct rigorous climate modeling
